# RC545a VIS Storage & Equipment Management

## Goals
- Keep all XR, AR and interaction devices organised and ready for use.
- Ensure a fast turnaround between sessions.
- Prevent loss of chargers, adapters and cables.
- Make booking and fault reporting simple.

---
## Storage Zones (Cupboard)

### **Shelf A: Headsets and AR glasses**
Contents:
- Vive Focus Vision
- Meta Quest units (x2)
- XREAL Air 2 Ultra
- XREAL Beam Pro
Storage rules:
- Each device stored in a labelled bin.
- Chargers and cables must remain inside the bin.
- Use asset IDs (eg: `VIS-HMD-Q2-01`).

### **Shelf B: Tracking and sensors**
Contents:
- Vive Trackers (x2)
- Leap Motion 2
- Realsense/Einscan (once added)
Rules:
- Trackers stored with straps.
- Keep USB cables tied with velcro loops.

### **Shelf C: Interaction and prototyping**
Contents:
- Makey Makey kits
- 3DConnexion SpaceNavigator
- Misc USB hubs and controllers
Rules:
- Kits stored intact, avoid mixing parts between kits.

### **Shelf D: Networking, power, spares**
Contents:
- GL.iNet router
- Extension leads
- Spare USB-C, USB-A, HDMI, DP cables
Rules:
- Cables labelled and hung on hooks.

## Charging Station Setup
Location: desk with workstation PC

Includes:
- Multi-port USB-C charger
- Labelled charging cables (never leave this area)
- Tray for charging headsets and trackers

Rules:
- Devices must be charged before returning to the cupboard.
- Use “Ready” or “Needs Charging” magnets or tags.
---

## Booking and Status Tags
Status conventions:
- **Green:** Available
- **Yellow:** Booked or in active use
- **Red:** Fault / missing part

Fault reporting:
- QR code linking to a simple form
- Example log entry: `VIS-HMD-Q2-01 – Right controller drift`
